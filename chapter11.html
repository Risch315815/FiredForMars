Chapter 11: Degeneration

Changa drank 3 bottles of water during the 5-minute presentation showcasing the new flagging model developed by the Media and Emotional Stability Department. Officer Abdul seemed concerned about his well-being.

However, he did complete the presentation concisely.

Officer Abdul was waiting for the board members to come to a conclusion again. "The board sincerely requests that the Media and Emotional Stability Department develop an updated model capable of parsing real-time surveillance footage for deviant conduct."
He paused. "Could such request be fulfilled within 2 months?"

Changan was chugging in his fifth bottle of water at that moment. He finished the bottle, wiped his mouth. "We would do our best. If more time is needed, we would submit a request 2 weeks before the deadline."

"Very well. Meeting adjourned."

Jimmy waited outside the restroom for Changan.
5 minutes later, Changan finally finished.

"They didn't even say what it's for." Jimmy whispered beside him.
"Well, anything for the good of the colony, right?" Changan said. "Let's go."

Brendan stopped eating lunch with Changan.

Instead, Changan found a USB drive under table #13, stuck to the under surface of the lunch table with a blu tack. It held compressed dev logs, snippets of code, and one-word filenames like BLUEPRINT, NEEDLE, and GHOST.
Changan left his replies in the same spot: architecture notes, alternate loss functions, innocuous changelogs buried in folder trees.

On the outside, they barely acknowledged each other.
Inside, the patch took shape.

The modified model was completed in time. It was leaner, faster, optimized for CCTV input.

The board suggested that Brendan Hertz, Chief officer of Power Allocation Department examine the model.
Brendan waited a week, then returned it with minor syntax edits, suggested metadata tags, and one flagged misclassification: a passerby helping a fallen senior citizen up, misread as a physical altercation.

After corrections, the patch was launched.

Brendan set the secondary patch to release in 14 days.

Changan opened the terminal to Brendan's message in the USB drive:
"Project Blindspot: trial
- Timing: 2125/05/05, 12:30 (22th day after its launch)
- Place: Section H, beside the old oxygen exchange pipe
- Action: incremental, with deniability
-- Trial #1: Ch drop wallet, Br pick it up, grab Ch's shoulder to notice him. (5-10 sec)
-- Trial #2: Ch hide a gift behind the corner, blindfold Brendan, hold his hands and lead him to the present. (20-30 sec)
-- Trial #3: Br act like he is not physically fit, and pretend to faint. Ch hold him in his arms. Br recover gradually. (2-3 min)
- Verification: Wait three days after each trial. No alerts = proceed. Delayed alert = abort."

Changan stared at the screen, mesmerized.
Still couldn't believe a man so vibrant yet so precise fell for him. It felt like a dream on the verge of becoming a nightmare.

He couldn't stop wavering.

They waited a full week before Trial #3, because Changan needed to be sure.
They met at the designated spot. Nodded. Began.

Brendan walked unevenly, looking at his phone. Changan waved, unacknowledged. Then Brendan collapsed. Changan caught him just in time.
He checked for breath and pulse. He let Brendan stay in his arms for another two minutes.
Finally, he whispered, "You can wake up now."
Brendan stirred, then buried his face in Changan's tummy. Changan blushed.

Brendan stood. Changan looped his arm over his shoulder, supporting him as they walked.

Then, around the corner—

Brendan pressed Changan against the wall, caging him with both arms.
"Impeccable performance, Director Wang," he said, eyes gleaming like a predator tasting blood.
"W.. we should wait until Trial #3 is verified..." Changan stammered.
"No need. The writing's on the wall," Brendan replied, pressing closer.

Changan ducked, slipping free. "We wait. Okay?"
Brendan hesitated. Then nodded.

They returned to work.

During the monthly board update, Chief Secretary of the Safety and Stability Office mentioned that the new model had stabilized at 88% efficiency.
The board members applauded this achievement, except one.

"I move to raise a concern."
"Granted." Said Abdul.
"Officer Brendan Hertz was a principal contributor to the previous model. His assessment may carry unconscious bias."
Silence.
"The current model is effective," someone countered. "Reexamination will cause disruption."
"It would be a limited audit within five workdays. We'll revert to the old model in the interim."
He glanced at the rest of the board members. "For the long term good of the colony."

Abdul looked around. "All in favor?"
Unanimous.
"Motion approved."

An exhausted flagging officer rubbed his eyes. Since the rollback, his workload had doubled. Maybe Tripled.
"I HATE big government." He muttered.

Another red flag alert. He growled, expecting to see someone standing too close to another person for too long.
"I'll be out of here in a week. Next time I rotate to this post would be 2 years later." He comforted himself.

Instead, his eyes widened.
There were two man kissing. A man's hand was wrapped around the other man's neck, and the other man is rubbing that man's crutch.
Face recognition system said: Brendan Hertz, Chief Director of Power Allocation Department; Changan Wang, Chief Director of Media and Emotional Stability Department.

He closed the window, drank a whole bottle of coffee, then stared at the screen for a while.
He backed up the surveillance footage, saved it to a USB drive, deleted the log, and rushed to his superior's office with the USB driver.

Brendan and Changan was locked up in detention cells next to each other. The silence dripped onto them like wet concrete, crushing their every bone slowly.

Changan closed his eyes.

Brendan looked down.
"Maybe next time, we don't try it... on ourselves."
