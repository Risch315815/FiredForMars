Chapter 13: The Post You Should Have Ignored

When developing the new flagging model, the Media and Emotional Wellbeing Department decided on saving the wildest conspiracy posts for "manual inspection": it had been their only entertainment outlet during office hours.

Changan got a "Conspiracy Theory Post Alert" in the late afternoon. He was excited.

"Come on, come on, come on, get daddy the good stuff~" he muttered as he opened the post.

"The government is experimenting on patients with rare cancer at Cronenberg Memorial Hospital with novel therapies that can cause severe side effects? Bo~ring~ Am I supposed to believe these AI slop monstrosities are real cancer patients? Kids these days lack originality." He spun lazily in his office chair, mocking the text.

Still, he read through the whole post before censoring it. Synovial sarcoma? Clear-cell carcinoma? Malignant peripheral nerve sheath tumors? He probably asked a chatbot for the 10 rarest cancers or something.

He flagged it, deleted it, and went home.
Had a good night's sleep.

The next morning before work, Changan received a message:

"Dear Media and Emotional Wellbeing Officers, the department office underwent a gas leak last night; luckily no one was harmed. However, the office, including the database center, will be shut down for at least three weeks for repairs; data access will be restricted. Please enjoy your paid time off and patiently wait for further notice."

Joy surged over him. He walked through his morning routine at half his normal speed.

But the censored post and this morning's notice stuck in his throat like a hairball. He scribbled down what he remembered about the post: "Conanberg Memorial Hospital", "Synthetic Coma", "Clear Cell Cancer", "Malignant..." whatever. The name's too long.
He convinced himself to let the feeling go and returned to his fluffy paid day-off.

9:20 a.m. Nothing to do. Nothing to think about.
He strolled into the living room area and saw the pot of tea still sitting on the table.
Were they ever good for each other? Or was that what Brendan tried to make him think?
Changan was still messy inside, so he decided to give himself more time to think.

A few days later during lunch, Changan bumped into Sam and Jimmy in the food court.

"Hey guys, how have you been?" asked Changan.
"It's awesome! I can sleep in every day and play 'When Empires Rise' as long as I want!"
Changan frowned. Wasn't that game pure neo-libertarian propaganda? Also, it was made hastily in 40 days to fill a quota, 30 years ago, by the old Media and Wellbeing Department.
Guess Brendan was right. These boys are dumb.

"Oh, have you heard? Mr. Hertz was sick. We're going to visit him in the hospital this afternoon, are you coming, Mr. Wang?"
Changan wasn't ready for this at all; he could only wave his hands haphazardly and mutter, "Oh well I have something to do or something like that. You should just go without me, hehehe..."
Sam and Jimmy looked at each other, confused. But eventually, they just stood up and said goodbye to Changan before they left.
"What was that?" Jimmy asked. "I think they had a fight," Sam speculated.

Changan heard everything they whispered. He rolled his eyes.

3 weeks into his paid vacation, Changan kept bumping into snacks he thought Brendan would have liked in the canteen. He finally decided to share his exploration with Brendan. As a friend.

"I found some snacks you might like, should we meet during lunch tomorrow?" He wrote the message in the most casual way possible, after rewriting his draft a thousand times.
"I'm still in the hospital."

Changan didn't want to bother the nurses rushing in and out of wards, so he took his time looking for Brendan's room.

He knocked, opened the door, but immediately apologized and tried to close it.
"Ching chang."
His body froze, one hand still on the doorknob. Eyes trembling.
Then he stepped inside, shutting the door behind him with a quiet click.
His tears came fast, silently. He turned his face away from the hall.
Brendan sat on his bed, waiting for Changan to cry himself dry. Then he smiled, opening his arms for Changan.

"I've thought about it. It would be pretty cool to be a cancer survivor." Brendan said as he crunched on Changan's snack. "They said I have 'clear-cell carcinoma' in my kidney. Such an beautiful name, sounds like crystal."
Changan still couldn't say anything, just tightly holding his cold, papery hand. He couldn't see any Brendan left in this skeleton sitting in front of him.
"I'll be going to Cronenberg Memorial Medical Center tomorrow. Hope the food's better there." He put another chip into his mouth.
"I used to think all I needed was a perfect algorithm. But when the boys brought me snacks... and talked to me... " Brendan's voice cracked. "They made me so happy."
Changan's sobs returned, his whole body trembling. Brendan wrapped an arm around him and rubbed slow, quiet circles on his back.
"You are a patient and tender person. You deserve someone who sees you and makes you happy."
Changan shook his head with tears in his eyes.
"I'm here for you." Brendan said, with tears rolling down his face. "As long as I can."

Changan stood by the window, replaying what Brendan said over and over again in his head. He couldn't tell which Brendan was the real one: the scheming yet charismatic devil, or the vulnerably genuine skeleton he met this afternoon.
Whichever it was, Brendan was not here to figure it out with him.

All of a sudden, something hit him. He grabbed his scribble of that post that shut down the whole department: "Conanberg Memorial". "Clear Cell Cancer".

Brendan was in danger.

The next morning, he reached Youssef's office and scheduled a meeting immediately. Youssef picked up shortly after the AI assistant and scheduled their meeting right before office hours were over.

Changan entered the room. "There's this mutual friend of ours."

Youssef cursed, pressed a button. "Room is clear. Go on."

"3 weeks age, I received a post reporting the human experiments at Cronenberg Memorial Hospital, and the post mentioned a list of rare cancers. The next day, the Media and Emotional Wellbeing Department was shut down and denied database access till this day." Changan explained. "Brendan was diagnosed with one of the cancers on that list, and he was sent to Cronenberg Memorial Hospital this morning."

Youssef was furious. "Those bastards!" He immediately called the director of Cronenberg Memorial Hospital with the intercommunication system. "Director of Power Allocation Office, Brendan Hertz, his previous project requires his oversight. Please return him with full function at once."

The next day, Brendan was transferred back to the original hospital. The doctor told him he was at Cronenberg Memorial Hospital for its exclusive diagnostic equipment, though Brendan didn't remember having any test.

Six weeks and several resection surgeries later, he was cured and discharged.
